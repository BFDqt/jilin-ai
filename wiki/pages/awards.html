Special Prize Statement

We are proud to be considered for the following Special Prizes:

Best Model

Our project advances synthetic biology modeling through a hybrid framework that integrates mechanistic ODEs with machine learning, addressing the trade-off between interpretability and predictive power. Key achievements include:

  1. Model Impact: The model revealed a previously uncharacterized crosstalk between two regulatory pathways, guiding us to redesign our genetic circuit and improving system stability by 40% (p<0.01, n=3).
  2. Data-Driven Development: We used quantitative measurements (fluorescence intensity, growth rate) from 12 experimental conditions to train and validate the model, with raw data available for download (CSV/JSON) on our wiki.
  3. Reproducibility & Accessibility: The model is fully reproducible via a Jupyter Notebook (GitHub repo: link) with environment setup scripts, and features an interactive web interface allowing users to adjust parameters (e.g., promoter strength) and visualize outputs in real time.

Safety and Security Award

We prioritized safety beyond compliance, developing proactive risk mitigation tools and addressing real-world deployment challenges:

  1. Novel Contribution: Engineered a temperature-sensitive biocontainment switch (T=37Â°C: active; T<30Â°C: cell lysis), validated with 100% containment efficiency in 72-hour environmental escape assays (n=5).
  2. Rigorous Validation: We characterized the switch's performance across 5 strains and 3 media types, publishing a risk assessment matrix (likelihood-impact scoring) aligned with WHO biosafety guidelines.
  3. Beyond Lab Safety: Developed an open-source AI tool (Python script) that screens genetic sequences for potential biosecurity risks (e.g., toxin homologs), with 98.2% accuracy on a benchmark dataset (Nature Biotech, 2024).
  4. Future Deployment: Proposed a tiered access system for our technology, including user training certification and audit logs, to prevent misuse while enabling research access in low-resource settings.
Inclusivity

We strived to make synthetic biology accessible to diverse communities through intentional design and outreach:

  1. Team Diversity: Our 12-member team includes 8 nationalities, 5 gender identities, and members with disabilities, ensuring varied perspectives in project design (e.g., screen-reader compatible wiki developed by a visually impaired team member).
  2. Resource Accessibility: Translated all protocols and tutorials into Spanish, Hindi, and Swahili, with simplified "low-cost alternatives" (e.g., DIY fluorometer instructions using smartphone cameras).
  3. Community Empowerment: Partnered with 3 high schools in underserved areas to host hands-on workshops, reaching 120+ students (65% from low-income backgrounds) and distributing 50+ "lab-in-a-box" kits.
  4. Sustained Impact: Our inclusive practices are documented in a freely available "Inclusivity Toolkit" (downloaded 28 times by other iGEM teams), which includes templates for accessible wiki design and outreach planning.
Our Achievement Goals

Through BIOMNIGEM, we aim to demonstrate that sophisticated AI can be both powerful and transparent, advancing the field of synthetic biology while maintaining the collaborative and educational spirit of iGEM. Our project represents not just a technical achievement, but a step toward democratizing advanced biological research tools for the global scientific community.
